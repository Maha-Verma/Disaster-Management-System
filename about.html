Earthquakes

Earthquakes are sudden, violent shaking of the ground caused by tectonic movements within the Earthâ€™s crust.

Signs:

  • Ground shaking.
  • Cracks appearing in walls or floors.

Causes:

  • Movement of tectonic plates.
  • Volcanic activity.
  • Human activities (e.g., mining).

Impacts:

  • Structural damage to buildings.
  • Landslides and tsunamis.

Do's:

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On during shaking.
  • Move away from windows and heavy objects.

Don'ts:

  • Do not run outside during shaking.
  • Avoid using elevators.

Volcanic Eruptions

Volcanic eruptions are explosive events where magma, gas, and ash are expelled from a volcano.

Signs:

  • Increase in seismic activity.
  • Unusual gas emissions.

Causes:

  • Magma rising to the surface.
  • Build-up of pressure within the Earth.

Impacts:

  • Destruction of habitats and property.
  • Airborne ash affecting air travel and respiratory health.

Do's:

  • Evacuate if advised by authorities.
  • Wear masks to avoid inhaling ash.

Don'ts:

  • Do not stay near a volcano during activity.
  • Avoid low-lying areas due to potential lava flows.

Tsunamis

Tsunamis are large ocean waves usually triggered by underwater earthquakes or volcanic eruptions.

Signs:

  • Rapid retreat of the sea from the shore.
  • Strong earthquake near coastal areas.

Causes:

  • Underwater earthquakes.
  • Volcanic eruptions or landslides under the sea.

Impacts:

  • Widespread coastal flooding.
  • Massive destruction of property and loss of life.

Do's:

  • Move to higher ground immediately after an earthquake.
  • Heed tsunami warnings from authorities.

Don'ts:

  • Do not go to the beach to watch the waves.
  • Avoid low-lying areas until the all-clear is given.

Hurricanes

Hurricanes are powerful tropical storms characterized by strong winds, heavy rain, and storm surges.

Signs:

  • High winds and heavy rainfall.
  • Storm surge along coastlines.

Causes:

  • Warm ocean waters fueling the storm.
  • Low-pressure systems in the atmosphere.

Impacts:

  • Widespread flooding and wind damage.
  • Disruption of power and communication networks.

Do's:

  • Evacuate if instructed to do so by authorities.
  • Secure your home and property.

Don'ts:

  • Do not stay in low-lying or flood-prone areas.
  • Avoid driving during the storm.

Tornadoes

Tornadoes are rapidly rotating columns of air that extend from thunderstorms to the ground, causing intense damage.

Signs:

  • Dark, often greenish skies.
  • Rotating, funnel-shaped cloud.

Causes:

  • Severe thunderstorms.
  • Cold and warm air masses colliding.

Impacts:

  • Severe damage to buildings and infrastructure.
  • Loss of life and injuries.

Do's:

  • Take shelter in a basement or interior room.
  • Protect your head and neck from debris.

Don'ts:

  • Do not stay in mobile homes.
  • Avoid windows during a tornado.

Droughts

Droughts are prolonged periods of abnormally low rainfall, leading to a shortage of water.

Signs:

  • Reduced water levels in rivers and lakes.
  • Dry, cracked soil.

Causes:

  • Extended periods of low rainfall.
  • High temperatures and excessive evaporation.

Impacts:

  • Crop failure and food shortages.
  • Water supply shortages.

Do's:

  • Conserve water and use it efficiently.
  • Plan for drought-resistant landscaping.

Don'ts:

  • Do not waste water on non-essential activities.
  • Avoid planting water-intensive crops.

Wildfires

Wildfires are uncontrolled fires that spread rapidly through vegetation, often fueled by dry conditions and strong winds.

Signs:

  • Visible flames or smoke.
  • Strong smell of burning.

Causes:

  • Lightning strikes.
  • Human activities, such as unattended campfires.

Impacts:

  • Destruction of forests and homes.
  • Air pollution and health hazards.

Do's:

  • Evacuate immediately if instructed.
  • Clear flammable materials from around your home.

Don'ts:

  • Do not start fires during dry conditions.
  • Avoid driving through smoke-filled areas.

Landslides

Landslides are the movement of rock, earth, or debris down a slope due to gravity, often triggered by rainfall or earthquakes.

Signs:

  • Cracks or bulges in the ground, roads, or pavements.
  • Doors or windows that stick or jam for the first time.

Causes:

  • Heavy rainfall or rapid snowmelt.
  • Earthquakes and volcanic activity.
  • Human activities like deforestation and construction.

Impacts:

  • Destruction of property and infrastructure.
  • Blockage of roads and rivers, leading to floods.

Do's:

  • Stay alert to warnings from authorities.
  • Move away from landslide-prone areas during heavy rainfall.

Don'ts:

  • Do not build near steep slopes or at the base of mountains.
  • Avoid driving in landslide-prone areas during heavy rain.

Avalanches

Avalanches are rapid flows of snow down a slope, often triggered by natural or human factors.

Signs:

  • Loud cracking or "whumping" sounds from the snow.
  • Recent avalanches in the area.

Causes:

  • Heavy snowfall or rapid warming.
  • Wind-drifted snow creating unstable layers.
  • Human activities such as skiing or snowboarding.

Impacts:

  • Burial and suffocation of individuals.
  • Destruction of infrastructure and natural environments.

Do's:

  • Check avalanche forecasts before going into the mountains.
  • Carry essential avalanche safety equipment (beacon, probe, shovel).

Don'ts:

  • Do not enter closed or warning-marked avalanche-prone areas.
  • Avoid stopping or resting in avalanche-prone slopes.
